Energy Consumption and Carbon Emissions of Modern Video Encoders
In this project, we provide a comparative study between five leading video coding standards, namely H.264/AVC, H.265/HEVC, H.266/VVC, VP9, and AV1 through their open-source and fast software encoders x264, x265, VVenC, libvpx-vp9, and SVT-AV1, respectively, in terms of quality, energy consumption and CO2 emissions. This study allows to better understand the trade-off between energy consumption, bitrate, and quality of different encoders and determines which one is the most appropriate for developing green and sustainable video streaming solution.
Evaluation System
The evaluation of modern video encoding standards passes through four steps

1. Original dataset

We select the joint video exploration team common test conditions (JVET-CTC) dataset.

2. Video encoding

We encode the JVET-CTC datset with with a set of target encoding parameters.
Codec : AVC, HEVC, VVC, VP9, and AV1
Bitrate : four selected bitrates

3. Evaluation metrics

We measuring the encoding time, energy consumption, the equivalence co2 emissions, percentage of CPU and memory usage. In addition, we assess the quality of the encoded videos by using PSNR, SSIM and VMAF.

4. Results and analysis

We analyze video encoding results for different codecs by using the evaluation metrics.
